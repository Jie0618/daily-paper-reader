## 摘要
我们提出了关于物理信息神经网络（PINNs）中用于学习偏微分方程（PDEs）解的 $H^{-1}$ 残差损失表述的理论见解。标准的 PINN 表述使用多项损失泛函，由基于 $L^2$ 残差并离散化为均方误差（MSE）的内部和边界损失项组成。这些项的量级不平衡会导致数值刚性现象，从而引起病态问题和收敛缓慢。在这项工作中，我们分析了在具有任意非零 Dirichlet 边界条件的椭圆型 PDE 背景下使用的 $H^{-1}$ 范数离散化。我们证明，与标准的 MSE 离散化相比，这些 $H^{-1}$ 离散化重新平衡了 PDE 损失，改善了条件数，并缓解了刚性效应。我们通过随机采样残差的算子级实验，以及针对 Poisson 方程和稳态不可压缩 Navier-Stokes 方程的 PINN 实验验证了我们的理论结果。这些实验证实了所提出的重新平衡方法对于椭圆型 PDE 以及更广泛的具有椭圆特性的问题的数值有效性。

## Abstract
We present theoretical insights into $H^{-1}$ residual loss formulations of physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) for learning solutions of partial differential equations (PDEs). Standard PINN formulations use a multi-term loss functional consisting of interior and boundary loss terms that are based on $L^2$-residuals and discretized as mean square errors (MSE). Imbalanced magnitudes of these terms cause numerical stiffness phenomena, resulting in ill-conditioning and slow convergence. In this work, we analyze discretizations of the $H^{-1}$-norm that are used in the context of elliptic PDEs with arbitrary, nonzero Dirichlet boundary conditions. We prove that these $H^{-1}$ discretizations rebalance the PDE loss, improve conditioning, and mitigate stiffness effects compared with the standard MSE discretization. We validate our theoretic results through operator-level experiments with randomly sampled residuals and PINN experiments for the Poisson and stationary incompressible Navier-Stokes equations. These experiments confirm the numerical effectiveness of the proposed rebalancing for elliptic PDEs and, more broadly, for problems with elliptic behavior.
