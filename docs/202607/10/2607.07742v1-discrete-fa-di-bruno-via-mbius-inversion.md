## 摘要
我们从莫比乌斯反演的角度研究离散和微分 Faà di Bruno 公式。在布尔立方体上，牛顿离散泰勒公式与迭代前向差分的定义构成了一对 zeta-莫比乌斯对偶；通过复合两个泰勒展开并进行一次反演，得到了固定基点处的闭式离散 Faà di Bruno 公式：对于阿贝尔群之间的任意映射 $f, g$，有 $Δ(f \circ g;\,x;\,u_1,\dots,u_k) = \sum_{H \in \mathrm{Cov}(k)} Δ(f;\,g(x);\,(Δ(g;x;u_T))_{T\in H})$，其中 $\mathrm{Cov}(k)$ 表示 $[k]$ 的非空子集覆盖。对重复方向进行分组可得到多重索引网格上的二项式版本，通过迭代可得到 $m$ 重复合映射的公式，其整数覆盖系数由显式的交叉和层级递归控制，这是 Constantine--Savits 公式的离散模拟。经典 Faà di Bruno 公式中出现的覆盖与划分之间的关系在代数背景下得到了展示。离散公式是布尔立方体函数代数上的泰勒展开，其幂等生成元吸收了重叠乘积；而在微分模拟中，幂零生成元消除了重叠，仅保留了划分。我们展示了如何将这些代数恒等式提升到 Banach 空间之间 $C^n$ 映射的分析背景中，从而恢复了 Constantine--Savits 的多元 Faà di Bruno 公式，并将其扩展到多个映射的复合。因此，布尔有限差分、二项式网格公式、无穷小泰勒代数和 Fréchet 导数呈现为一个莫比乌斯对偶 Faà di Bruno 公式的四种实现，并由一个平坦族连接。

## Abstract
We approach discrete and differential Faà di Bruno formulas from a Möbius inversion angle. On the Boolean cube, Newton's discrete Taylor formula and the definition of iterated forward differences form a zeta--Möbius dual pair, and composing two Taylor expansions and inverting once yields a closed discrete Faà di Bruno formula at a fixed basepoint: for arbitrary maps $f, g$ between abelian groups, $$ Δ(f \circ g;\,x;\,u_1,\dots,u_k) = \sum_{H \in \mathrm{Cov}(k)} Δ(f;\,g(x);\,(Δ(g;x;u_T))_{T\in H}), $$ where $\mathrm{Cov}(k)$ denotes the coverings of $[k]$ by nonempty subsets. Grouping repeated directions gives binomial versions on multi-index grids, and iterating gives formulas for $m$-fold composites, with integer covering coefficients governed by explicit cross and level recursions, a discrete analogue of the Constantine--Savits formulas.   The relationship between coverings and partitions appearing in classical Faà di Bruno formulas is exhibited in an algebraic setting. The discrete formulas are Taylor expansions over the function algebra of the Boolean cube, whose idempotent generators absorb overlapping products; in the differential analogue nilpotent generators annihilate overlaps and only partitions remain.   We demonstrate how these algebraic identities can be lifted to the analytical setting of $C^n$ maps between Banach spaces, recovering the multivariate Faà di Bruno formula of Constantine--Savits and extending it to composites of several maps. Boolean finite differences, binomial grid formulas, infinitesimal Taylor algebras, and Fréchet derivatives thus appear as four realizations of one Möbius-dual Faà di Bruno formula, connected by a flat family.
