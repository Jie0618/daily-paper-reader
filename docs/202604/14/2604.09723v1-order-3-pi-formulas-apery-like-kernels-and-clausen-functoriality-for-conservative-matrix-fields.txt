# Order-3 π-formulas, Apéry-like kernels, and Clausen functoriality for Conservative Matrix Fields 

Alex Shvets 

Abstract 

Raz, Shalyt, Leibtag, Kalisch, Weinbaum, Hadad, and Kaminer recently showed that formulas for π can be organized by canonical polynomial recurrences and partially unified by a rank-2

Conservative Matrix Field (CMF). We prove that each order-3 recurrence explicitly printed in the public Appendix B.6 of their paper is a shifted summation lift of an explicit order-2

kernel, and identify all three kernels: the two π-kernels are explicit rescalings of the sporadic Apéry-like sequences A036917 and A002895 (Domb numbers, case (α)), while the Catalan kernel is a hypergeometric twist of the Gauss-square coefficient sequence at (a, b, c ) = ( 12 , 1, 32 ). We place these kernels in a unified Sym 2 framework: the first π-kernel and the Catalan kernel come directly from Gauss-square coefficient sequences, while the Domb kernel is recovered by recasting the classical degree-3 Belyi pullback ϕ(x) = 108 x2/(1 − 4x)3 and the associated algebraic twist in CMF language. We write an explicit square-gauge matrix for the Gauss CMF, formulate the standard pullback–twist transport in CMF terms, and show that for rank-2 objects it is compatible with Sym 2. We further prove an inverse classification: for a fixed Sym 2-type Riemann scheme, the one-parameter family of Fuchsian operators contains a unique Sym 2( Gauss ) point, cut out by the closed-form condition λ0 = 2 γ1γ2(1 − 2α) on the accessory parameter. Finally, a Belyi-pullback scan over 5040 configurations produces 11 additional integer sequences of the form 

[xn]λn 2F1(a, b ; c; ϕ(x)) 2; we prove their integrality and place them in the same Sym 2-pullback framework. 

# 1 Introduction 

The recent preprint of Raz, Shalyt, Leibtag, Kalisch, Weinbaum, Hadad, and Kaminer develops an automated pipeline for harvesting, validating, canonicalizing, and partially unifying formulas for mathematical constants, with the flagship case study being π [14 ]. Applied to 385 validated formulas for π, their procedure produces 153 canonical polynomial recurrences: 149 of order 2 and 

4 of order 3 [ 14 , §3.3]. The same work exhibits a concrete rank-2 three-dimensional Conservative Matrix Field (CMF) for π and proves that 81 of the 153 canonical forms reside in that CMF [ 14 ,Table 4]. The appearance of order-3 canonical forms raises a natural structural question. Are these order-3 objects genuinely outside rank-2 CMF geometry, or can some of them be decomposed into lower-order kernels together with external summation? The public Appendix B.6 of [ 14 ] prints two explicit order-3 recurrences for π and one for Catalan’s constant. We apply a standard Ore-algebraic factorization criterion—right divisibility by (S − 1) , equivalent to the vanishing of the coefficient sum—to these three printed recurrences and show that each is a shifted summation lift of an order-2

kernel. Our second result identifies all three printed kernels. The first π-kernel is an explicit rescaling of the classical Apéry-like sequence A036917 [ 9, 13 ]; the second π-kernel is an explicit rescaling of the Domb numbers A002895 (case (α)) [ 12 , 10 ]; and the Catalan kernel is a hypergeometric twist of the coefficient sequence of 2F1( 12 , 1; 32 ; z)2. Our fourth result connects the ambient π-CMF to the classical theory. The third-order ODE for g(z) = 2F1(a, b ; c; z)2 was first derived by Chaundy [ 5] and is explicit in Vid¯ unas [ 17 , Eq. We then prove a general pullback–twist functoriality theorem: composing a CMF basis with a rational pullback and a scalar twist produces a new CMF whose shift and differential generators are given by explicit formulas, and Sym 2 commutes with this operation. This places all three printed kernels inside a single mechanism. We stress three limitations. First, Raz et al. canonicalize formulas at the level of partial sums or convergents ; our order-2 kernels live at the level of summands or coefficient sequences. Thus our results refine, rather than contradict, the formula-level minimal orders reported in [ 14 ]. Second, Raz et al. report four order-3 canonical forms for π, but only two explicit order-3 π-recurrences are printed in the public Appendix B.6; the fourth order-3 π-canonical form is not explicit in the public materials used here. Third, our intrinsic derivation of A036917 uses the differential component Mθ

of the ambient Gauss CMF. It remains open whether the same kernel appears as a trajectory of the current rank-2 π-CMF in the sense of parameter shifts. The paper is organized as follows. Section 2 recalls the minimal amount of CMF and canonical-form formalism needed below. Section 3 proves the factorization criterion and applies it to the three printed order-3 recurrences from Appendix B.6 of [ 14 ]. Section 4 identifies all three kernels, 2proves the general square-gauge formula for Gauss CMFs, derives the intrinsic differential equation and coefficient recurrence of the square, records the pure Clausen corollary, establishes an inverse classification via the accessory parameter, and closes the Domb branch via a Belyi pullback and a pullback–twist functoriality theorem for CMFs. Section 5 reports the results of a Belyi-pullback scan over 5040 parameter configurations, proves integrality for 11 additional integer sequences, and places them in the Sym 2-pullback framework. Section 6 lists consequences and open problems. 

# 2 Preliminaries 

2.1 Conservative Matrix Fields and trajectories 

We use the modern CMF formalism of [ 8 , 18 ]. A CMF is a 1-cocycle of Zd with values in matrices of rational functions. 

Definition 2.1. Let K be a field and let σv denote the shift action of v ∈ Zd on rational functions in d variables. A Conservative Matrix Field of dimension d and rank r over K is a map 

M : Zd → GL r(K(x)) , v 7 → M v,

such that 

Mv+w = Mv σv(Mw) (v, w ∈ Zd). (1) Following [18], if f is a D-finite function and B is a basis of the finite-dimensional Ore-module generated by f , then the basis-change matrices relating B and its parameter shifts form a CMF [18 , Theorem 3.1]. Moreover, changing the basis changes the CMF by a rational coboundary transformation [18, Proposition 3.2]. Given a basepoint x and a direction v ∈ Zd, one obtains a one-dimensional object by sampling along the trajectory x + nv . The corresponding trajectory matrix is 

Tx,v (n) := Mv(x + nv ).

When a rational gauge transform converts Tx,v (n) to companion form, the trajectory yields a linear recurrence in the step variable n. This is the bridge between CMFs and canonical polynomial recurrences used in [14, 18]. 

2.2 Canonical forms and coboundary equivalence 

Raz et al. associate to a formula for a constant its canonical form : the minimal linear recurrence with polynomial coefficients satisfied by the corresponding sequence of rational approximants [ 14 ,§3.3 and Appendix C.4]. In the second-order case this is equivalently represented as a polynomial continued fraction (PCF) [ 14 , §2.1 and Appendix E]. Throughout the paper we work in the Ore algebra 

Q[n]⟨S⟩,

where S is the forward shift and satisfies 

S p (n) = p(n + 1) S (p ∈ Q[n]) .

We also write σ for the induced automorphism σ(p)( n) = p(n + 1) .Two polynomial matrix recurrences A(n) and B(n) are coboundary equivalent if there exist a polynomial matrix U (n) and nonzero polynomials pA(n), p B (n) such that

# 6 Discussion and open problems 

The results above separate three levels of structure. Section 5 adds a second concrete output channel to the story. Beyond the three printed order-3

kernels, the same Belyi-pullback machinery yields 11 additional integer sequences from the 5040 -tuple 29 scan. Theorem 5.1 proves integrality for all eleven rows, Proposition 5.2 shows that they are again 

Sym 2-pullbacks of rank-2 Gauss objects, and Remark 5.3 shows that none of them hides an order-2

Apéry-like recurrence of low degree. At the formula level , Raz et al. correctly canonicalize partial sums or convergents and find minimal polynomial recurrences [ 14 ]. In that sense, the printed examples in Appendix B.6 are indeed order-3 formulas. At the kernel level , however, Theorem 3.3 shows that every order-3 recurrence explicitly printed in Appendix B.6 is obtained from an order-2 kernel by one summation. This is a structural refinement of the public order-3 examples, not a correction of the formula-level minimal orders in [14]. Theorem 4.1 identifies the first printed π-kernel with A036917 ; Theorem 4.3 identifies the second with the Domb numbers A002895 ; and Theorem 4.4 traces the Catalan kernel to the Gauss-square point ( 12 , 1, 32 ). Theorem 4.13 then closes the Domb branch by exhibiting the Domb generating function as a Belyi-pulled-back twisted Gauss square at ( 16 , 13 , 1) , and Theorem 4.14 establishes that pullback–twist preserves CMF structure. Consequently, all three printed order-3 kernels are covered by a single mechanism (Table 2): rank-2 2F1-object Sym 2

−−−−→ rank-3 module pullback/twist 

−−−−−−−−−→ kernel summation 

−−−−−−−→ order-3 formula. Theorem 4.11 provides an inverse classification. For a fixed Sym 2-type Riemann scheme, the local exponents do not determine the operator uniquely—one accessory parameter λ remains free. The Sym 2(Gauss ) locus is cut out by the single equation λ = 2 γ1γ2(1 − 2α), which recovers (a, b, c )

explicitly. This is not an algorithmic detection (cf. Singer [ 15 ] and van Hoeij [ 16 ] for operator-level symmetric-square tests) but a closed-form criterion on the accessory parameter. Its verification on the A036917 and Catalan cases (Remark 4.12) confirms the consistency of the direct and inverse approaches. We emphasize that the individual links in this chain are not new: the third-order ODE for f 2 is due to Chaundy [ 5 ] and Vid¯ unas [ 17 ]; the connection between Apéry-like sequences and symmetric squares of hypergeometric equations has been understood since Almkvist, van Straten, and Zudilin [ 1 ], and Gorodetsky [ 10 ] states explicitly that the generating functions of the Almkvist–Zudilin sequences are essentially the squares of the corresponding Zagier sequences; the connection between A036917 

and π-series is established by Chan–Verrill [ 2 ] and Cooper [ 7 ]; and the coefficient recurrence for general parameters is now in Mao–Tian [ 11 ]. The Belyi map ϕ(x) = 108 x2/(1 − 4x)3 and the Domb representation through a pulled-back Gauss square are known in the modular-forms literature [ 3 , 4 ]; the underlying pullback of differential equations is a standard construction [ 17 ]. Our contribution is threefold: the unified CMF reinterpretation of these classical links, the pullback–twist functoriality theorem for CMFs (Theorem 4.14, an elementary restatement of standard gauge transport in the CMF setting), and the inverse classification via the accessory parameter (Theorem 4.11). Several concrete problems remain open. (a) Raz et al. report four order-3 canonical forms for π, but only two explicit order-3 π-recurrences are printed in the public Appendix B.6. Determining whether the fourth public-unprinted 

order-3 π-canonical form is also a summation lift is the most immediate missing computation. (b) Theorems 4.8, 4.13, and 4.14 show that the printed kernels are intrinsic to the differential 

components of the ambient Gauss CMF and its pullback. It remains open whether any of them also appear as genuine trajectories of the current rank-2 π-CMF or of its square. (c) Theorem 4.14 handles rational pullbacks and scalar twists. Extending this to algebraic pullbacks with algebraic twists in full generality—and classifying which Belyi maps are compatible with Apéry-like integrality—would connect the present framework to the modular parametrizations underlying the sporadic sequences. 30 (d) More generally, one may ask for a classification of parameters (a, b, c ) and Belyi maps ϕ for which the coefficient sequence of u(x) · 2F1(a, b ; c; ϕ(x)) 2 is an Apéry-like sequence, and for which such kernels can be realized by CMF trajectories. (e) The 11 sequences of Section 5 raise a geometric and arithmetic problem of their own: identify the corresponding motives or period interpretations, determine the dessins attached to the Belyi maps 4x/ (1 − x)2 and 27 x/ (1 − 4x)3, and understand the associated L-functions. (f) It is also natural to ask whether the same 11 sequences satisfy Dwork-type congruences or stronger supercongruences at primes p, and whether these congruences can be read off directly from the pullback data (a, b, c, ϕ, λ ).(g) Mao and Tian [ 11 ] derive the coefficient recurrence for 2F1(a, b ; c; z)k for k = 2 and k = 3 . It would be interesting to extend this to general k and to determine whether the corresponding 

Sym k(CMF) construction yields further Apéry-like kernels for k ≥ 3.
