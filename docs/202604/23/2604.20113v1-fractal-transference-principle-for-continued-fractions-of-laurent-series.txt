FRACTAL TRANSFERENCE PRINCIPLE FOR CONTINUED FRACTIONS OF LAURENT SERIES 

YUTO NAKAJIMA 

Abstract. We establish a fractal transference principle for continued fraction expansions over the field of Laurent series. Let S be an infinite subset of the set of all polynomials over a finite field of q elements of positive degree with growth density exponent őĪ ‚Č• 1, and let U ‚äā S be a subset of positive relative upper density. We prove that there exists a subset ES,U of the set of points whose continued fraction digits are pairwise distinct and belong to S such that dim H ES,U = 12őĪ .

Moreover, the set of digits appearing in the continued fraction expansions of points in ES,U recovers the relative upper density of U in S. We also show that the same construction preserves the relative upper density of the corresponding degree sets in N. As a consequence, combinatorial statements for subsets of N of positive upper density can be transferred to degree sets arising from continued fraction expansions of Laurent series on sets of optimal Hausdorff dimension. 

1. Introduction

More remarkably, Green and Tao [11] showed that even the prime numbers contain arithmetic progressions of arbitrary length.
