# Optimality conditions via exact penalty functions 

## Vsevolod Ivanov Ivanov Department of Mathematics, Technical University of Varna, 9010 Varna, Bulgaria February 23, 2026 

Abstract 

In this paper, we obtain optimality conditions for the problem with inequality, equality and closed set constraints in terms of the lower Hadamard derivative. The results are obtained applying exact penalty functions. 

Keywords: local minimum, isolated local minimum, nonsmooth analysis; non-smooth optimization; generalized convex functions; optimality conditions; lower Hadamard directional derivative; 

AMS 2000 Mathematics Subject Classification codes: 49J52, 90C46, 26B25, 49K27, 90C26 

# 1 Introduction 

s1 

Optimality conditions play important role in optimization. In this article we obtain optimality conditions of Fritz John type for the problem with inequality, equality and a set constraints via exact penalty functions. The results are given in terms of lower Hadamard derivative. Quadratic penalty functions were originally applied in McShane McS73 

[10] for problems with continuously differentiable data. Our results use the constraint qualification ( 6

1). Similar assumption appeared in Demyanov, Di Pillo and Facchinei dem98 

[3] and references therein. Both assumptions coincide in the case when the problem has no inequality and set constraints. We cite the following papers where optimality conditions were obtained in terms of the lower Hadamard derivative or the respective subdifferential. Optimality conditions are given in Jimenez and Novo jim02 

[6], Luu and Nguen luu05 

[7]. Sufficient conditions for the problem with a set constraint are given in Penot pen84 

[11, Proposition 5.7(a)], for the problem with inequality constraints and a set constraint - in Ward war88 

[13, Corollary 3.1]. We refer to Ioffe iof84 

[8, Proposition 6] where ”fuzzy” Lagrange multiplier rule were obtained in terms of the lower Hadamard subdifferential under the name Dini subdifferential. Necessary optimality conditions were derived in Glover, Craven glo94 

[5, Theorem 2.1] using the approxi-mate subdifferential which is constructed with the help of the lower Hadamard derivative. Another Hadamard directional derivative is used in optimality conditions for set-valued optimization, but it is constructed there with the help of the limits of multifunctions in the sense of Painlev´ e-Kuratowski. This derivative differs from the usual lower Hadamard derivative in the single-valued scalar case. 1
